Cold-start note

Welcome — this is a cold start

Normal is opening your library without prior scan history, so this first pass is the slowest it will ever be. Later runs feel warmer because they reuse what the app already learned.

While that first pass warms up, here is the short version:

  • A scan reads file metadata, not the contents of your media.
  • The first scan is the slow one; results fill in as the cache warms.
  • OMDB and TMDB keys are optional. They add richer matching, but nothing breaks without them.
  • Nothing on disk changes until you confirm a fix yourself.
  • Your saved settings and prior scans make later launches faster.

Close this whenever you are ready. The workbench keeps warming up behind it.

Safe lock active

Hands off while ffmpeg works

A remux is rewriting one of your files on disk right now. Until it finishes, the workbench stays locked so nothing — not a second job, not a fresh scan, not a stray click — can touch that file mid-write and leave it half-rebuilt.

This is the one moment normal genuinely cannot be interrupted. Almost everything else in the app is preview-only or reversible; an in-flight remux is neither. So we pause the whole surface and let the single job run clean.

  • The Preview stays live. Watch the progress wash on the file being rebuilt to gauge how far along it is.
  • Scanning, fixes, and settings are paused, not cancelled — they come back exactly as you left them.
  • Nothing else on disk is changing. Only the file inside this remux is being written.
  • The lock lifts on its own the moment ffmpeg reports done. There is nothing here you need to click.

Larger files and slower disks make this take longer. It is still working even when the fill looks still.
